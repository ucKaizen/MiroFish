Executive Brief

A POC exploring AI-generated audience insights — virtual panels, synthetic audiences, digital-twin viewers
The exercise. Can AI generate useful audience-response metrics and qualitative insight without running a live panel? The category goes by several names — virtual panel, synthetic audience, digital-twin viewer — and is still forming. This proof-of-concept tests the idea on UK media content using MiroFish, an open-source engine that turns a structured audience description into persona-driven AI agents and a simulated audience response.

What we wanted to know

  • Can a small simulated panel produce directionally useful reach, engagement, appreciation, and clarity signals?
  • Can it surface marketable insights and risks pre-launch — the qualitative colour real panels deliver weeks later?
  • Where does it work, and where does it fail loudly enough to matter?

Sample evaluation

  • 6-panelist UK seed vs BBC One Sherwood Series 2 (25 Aug 2024)
  • Reach 6/6 · Engagement 5/6 · Appreciation 83 (good band) · Clarity flag 3/6
  • Directionally aligned with Series 1 reception
  • Surfaced an actionable promo idea and a political-tone risk
  • Every metric audits back to a per-panelist quote

Production opportunities

  • Pre-launch read on creative, casting, scheduling, regional fit
  • Risk rehearsal for polarising content before public exposure
  • Synthetic qualitative alongside quantitative measurement
  • Narrative layer over BARB / comScore / NIQ data
  • Once calibrated to historical outcomes — a cheap directional companion to the measurement stack
Status. POC complete on a single illustrative show. The output is useful as pre-launch hypothesis and qualitative colour, not yet as calibrated measurement. Production positioning depends on grounding the seed in real panel + media data and closing the validation loop — covered in the slides ahead.

The Simulation Target

A real BBC One Sunday drama — predictable against actual BARB outcomes

The target

Sherwood — Series 2
BBC One · Sunday 25 August 2024, 21:00 · 6 episodes · real launch
  • Writer: James Graham
  • Cast: Morrissey, Manville, Dolan, Lindsay, Dillane — ensemble, no household-name lead
  • Setting: Ex-mining Nottinghamshire, post-1984 Miners' Strike shadow
  • Tone: Slow-burn political thriller, demanding, emotionally weighty

The calibration question: can a 6-persona simulation built from 18 August viewing patterns predict Sherwood S2's actual audience response — Reach, Engagement, AI, Clarity — closely enough to be useful pre-launch? Output is fractions of the simulated cohort, not population projections. Real Sherwood S2 numbers exist — the simulation can be checked.

How the Simulation Works

OASIS multi-round agents on two parallel simulated platforms — and what that does and doesn't tell us

1 · Agents loaded

6 panelist personas + activity config (stance, sentiment bias, posting rate, active hours) generated from the knowledge graph.
▼
×N rounds (default 3)

2 · Per-round simulation loop

Each round, every agent reads the current feed, picks one action, and writes back. Both platforms run in parallel — agents on each see only that platform.
Reddit-style Post · comment · like · dislike · follow · search · refresh
Twitter-style Post · repost · quote · like · follow
↻ Each round's posts feed the next round's reading
▼

3 · ReportAgent aggregates

After N rounds, the ReportAgent reads the post log + graph, computes the four headline metrics, and synthesises the narrative report.

What the simulation captures

  • Vocal social-media discourse — what panelists would say
  • Persona-driven reactions across ages, regions, ethnicities
  • Narrative emergence — which themes spread, which divide
  • Risk surfacing — what could backfire pre-launch (cheap)
  • Synthetic qualitative interviews on demand

Limitations of this approach

  • The silent majority — only ~5–10% of any audience posts publicly
  • Actual viewing behaviour — did they tune in? Watch through? Drop off?
  • Purchase actions — did the campaign move sales?
  • No calibration — predictions have no error bars without ground truth
  • Reddit-shape ≠ population-shape — captures vocal discourse, not the market
Reddit/Twitter dynamics ≠ consumer behaviour. The simulation models what people would say if they posted, not what people would do. Useful for hypothesis generation and creative pre-flight; not a replacement for measured outcomes. The next two slides show how this changes when real panel + media + purchase data closes the loop.

The shift: today's pipeline is LLM at stages 2–5 with no validation — useful as a sandbox, not as a measurement instrument. The production version contains the LLM to stage 4 and closes the 6 → 3 retrain loop, inverting the LLM exposure ratio from ~80% to ~10–15% and making every prediction falsifiable.

The takeaway isn't "now I have a working simulator." It's "I now know which problems LLMs solve cheaply (narrative, exploration) and which they don't (calibration, prediction with error bars)." MiroFish has earned its place as a sandbox for understanding pipeline shape and failure modes; the production version reuses parts of it (OASIS, ReportAgent) but re-architected around real data and a validation loop.

Interesting findings

  • Marketable insight (Tom): directly proposes interactive promo — live discussions, social-media challenges. Useful actionable signal for marketing.
  • Risk flag (Klaus): warns the political tone could alienate "if too polarising." A specific creative-direction risk.
  • AI of 83 is plausible: Sherwood S1 reportedly scored similarly per BARB. Directionally sensible.
  • Clarity split: 3/6 flag unclear — Raj, Tom, Klaus. Demographic mix: not just young/old. Suggests pacing rather than age is the issue.
  • False positives still leaking: caveat The full report also contained quotes from "Laura Kuenssberg" and "Austrian" — agents the LLM mis-extracted as panelists. Quotes discarded. The structural fix is to declare the panel rather than extract it; pinning a name allowlist was considered and rejected as it papers over the underlying graph-extraction non-determinism.
Calibration check available: Sherwood S2 actually aired on 25 August 2024 — real BARB AI, reach, and review consensus exist. The next step is comparing this prediction against the real outcome to calibrate the simulator's reliability. Without that comparison, this output remains directional.
