# BBC One Sunday Panel — Study Context (lite)

This file is context for the panelist table. Nothing in this file is a simulation participant. The 6 simulation participants are in the separate panelists file. Programmes, themes, ratings, and rules listed here are reference material — not entities that post.

## Study overview

On Sunday 18 August 2024, a recruited panel of 6 UK viewers logged their viewing across BBC One, breakfast through late evening. Each panelist recorded which programmes they watched, how much (less than half / about half / more than half), an appreciation score on the BBC AI scale (0–100), the appreciation themes the programme delivered, and whether the message felt unclear.

The panel's behaviour on 18 August is the basis for predicting how each will respond to a new programme launching the following Sunday (25 August 2024) at 21:00.

## The six appreciation themes (attributes, not entities)

- informative — gave the panelist factual knowledge they didn't have before
- reflected UK life — felt authentic to contemporary Britain
- representative — portrayed a range of UK communities fairly
- culturally significant — felt important beyond entertainment
- thought-provoking — encouraged reflection
- emotionally engaging — moved the panelist

A panelist may flag that a programme's message was unclear.

## The Sunday schedule (reference, not participants)

BBC One's 18 August 2024 schedule: Breakfast (morning news), Sunday with Laura Kuenssberg (political), Sunday Worship (religion), BBC News at Six, Countryfile (rural affairs), a popular factual entertainment slot, The Weakest Link (quiz), the 21:00 prestige drama, BBC News at Ten, Match of the Day 2 (late sport).

These programmes are content the panelists watched. They are NOT simulation participants and must not appear as posting voices.

## Simulation task

The 6 panelists in the panelists file are the only simulation participants. The objective is to predict how each will respond to a new programme launching the following Sunday at 21:00. The specific programme is described in the prediction prompt.

For each panelist, predict:

1. Whether they will watch (and how much): less than half / about half / more than half / not at all.
2. Their appreciation score (0–100) — anchored in their 18 August rating pattern.
3. Which appreciation themes they will tag.
4. Whether they will flag the message as unclear.
