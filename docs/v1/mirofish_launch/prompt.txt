Predict how a panel of ten named technology evaluators will react to the
public launch of MiroFish v2 on Hacker News on Tuesday 12 May 2026.

The uploaded files describe (1) the product, (2) the ten evaluators with
their voice registers, recorded behaviour, required and forbidden
vocabulary, and (3) the launch context — discovery surfaces, topical
interests, and the action menu the runner uses.

For each evaluator, decide one action per round (DO_NOTHING, LIKE_POST,
REPOST, CREATE_POST, REPLY), grounded in their recorded behaviour and
the discovery channels where they peak. If they CREATE_POST or REPLY,
write the post in their voice — respect their length range, voice
register, required vocabulary, and forbidden vocabulary.

Run two rounds:
- Round 1 — exposure: each evaluator decides whether they see the
  launch on launch day, given their slot propensities.
- Round 2 — delayed exposure / reaction: evaluators with higher
  delayed_exposure_propensity (Felipe, Wei, Diego, Karim) may react in
  round 2 even if they did nothing in round 1.

Constrain every prediction to the ten named evaluators. Do not invent
features that are not described in the product overview. Distinguish
between MiroFish v2 (the product being launched) and the BBC One
Sherwood S2 example study it ships with — reactions should be to the
product, not the example.

The output report should contain:
- A headline metric block: how many of the ten engage, how many post,
  how many stay silent.
- A per-evaluator block with the action they took each round and any
  posts they wrote.
- A short narrator section in plain English ("strategy desk tone")
  summarising what the panel collectively signals about the launch.
